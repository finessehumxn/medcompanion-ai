Trust & data

Your health data stays yours.

MedCompanion is built local-first: your information lives on your phone, the AI is optional, and your clinician stays in charge. Here's exactly how it works — and how to check it for yourself.

📱 Local-first ✨ AI-optional 🩺 The human decides

The promises

📱 It stays on your phone

Your check-ins and records are saved only in your phone's local storage. They aren't uploaded when you open the app.

✨ AI sees data only when you tap

Nothing goes to the AI until you choose a feature. Then only the text for that one task is sent, to generate your answer.

🗄️ We store nothing by default

The AI features are stateless — they answer and forget. Your health content is not written to our database.

🧠 Never used to train AI

Your data is not used to train models, and never sold or used for advertising. Full stop.

What the AI will never do

  • Never diagnose you or decide your treatment.
  • Never replace your doctor's judgment — it gives information, not medical advice.
  • Never train AI models on your data.
  • Never sell your data or use it for ads.

MedCompanion helps you organize and understand your own health information so visits go further. It is not a medical device and does not provide diagnosis.

You're in control

  • The app's core works with AI turned completely off.
  • Nothing is sent until you tap an AI feature — and we tell you exactly what will be sent.
  • You can delete everything on your device, any time, in one tap.

For hospitals & compliance teams

Where we are — honestly

The architecture above is real and live today: local-first, single-vendor for text AI (Anthropic), and no server-side storage of health content by default. That already removes most of the data-exposure surface teams worry about.

The formal assurances a hospital needs are a separate, in-progress track: signed Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with each AI vendor, zero-data-retention configuration, a HIPAA compliance program, and a SOC 2 audit. We'd rather tell you exactly where that stands than overstate it. Reach out and we'll share our current data-flow documentation and roadmap.

MedCompanion provides information to help you understand your health and prepare for care. It is not a diagnosis, not medical advice, and does not replace your clinician. In an emergency, call your local emergency number.
