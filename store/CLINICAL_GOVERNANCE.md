# Clinical Governance — how many medical professionals do we actually need?

**The honest truth first:** for a general health-*information* app, there is **no government "medically approved" stamp** (that exists for FDA-cleared *devices*, which we are intentionally not). "Medically approved" here means **clinical credibility + a documented review process** — earned with a small, credentialed team, not a license.

## The one role that matters most
- **1 Medical Director** — a licensed, ideally board-certified physician (MD/DO) who **owns clinical accuracy and patient-safety** and signs off on the content/safety logic. This single role is the real "stamp." Many trusted health platforms run on a Medical Director + a small reviewer panel.

## Realistic team by stage
| Stage | Who | Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Launch / credible to investors** | 1 Medical Director + your 2 advisory physicians | **3** (you already have 2) |
| **Strong / well-rounded** | Add a **PharmD (pharmacist)** for the medication & interaction features and an **RN or NP** for everyday/triage tone | **~4–5** |
| **Scaling the "Reviewed by a real doctor" badge** | Enough reviewers to cover **volume, turnaround, key specialties, and the states your users are in** | grows with usage |

## So: the number
- **Minimum to credibly say "clinically reviewed / medically advised": 1 (a Medical Director).**
- **Realistic, well-rounded board: 3–5** — and you already have **2 physicians** committed, so you are most of the way there. Adding a **PharmD** is the highest-value next addition because of the medication/pharmacist features.
- The per-state, larger reviewer pool only matters **as the co-sign feature scales** — start with the board and grow with demand.

## Optional third-party seals (not required to launch)
- **URAC** accreditation (health content / telehealth) is a recognized third-party seal you can pursue later for extra trust; it costs money and time and is not needed to launch.
- Editorial-style credibility (named clinician reviewers / "medically reviewed by Dr. ___, MD" bylines, like Healthline/Mayo) is cheaper and immediately persuasive — your co-sign feature already does this.

## What to put in front of investors
> "We have a Medical Director (or are appointing one) plus a Medical Advisory Board of [N] licensed physicians, and every clinical surface goes through a documented physician review process. We are not an FDA device, so no clearance is required; clinical credibility comes from this governance, not a government stamp."
